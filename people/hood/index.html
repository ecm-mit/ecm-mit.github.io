Thesis: Lithium and Sodium Solid Electrolytes for Next-Generation Batteries: Bridging Mechanistic Understanding and Their Performance
BSc, Chemistry with Biochemistry, Wake Forest University, 2013

Zach Hood’s research focuses on the development of solid-state materials for next-generation batteries, catalysts for solar-to-fuel conversion, and nanomaterials for different energy-related applications. In his work, he develops new synthetic methods to produce next-generation materials with improved and novel properties. His research interest also includes the development of materials for sensors, biomedicine, and waste-to-energy conversion.
