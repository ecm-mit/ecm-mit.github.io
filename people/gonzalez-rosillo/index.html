Juan Carlos Gonzalez-Rosillo’s research is focused in the study of the Resistive Switching (RS) properties of Lithium-based materials for non-volatile memory applications and neuromorphic computing. Understanding the microscopic mechanism governing the RS effect in these materials and at the interfaces is crucial to the implementation of this technology in the emerging field of nanoelectronics. His expertise lies in the growth of functional thin films from both chemical and physical methods, microfabrication techniques and physical properties characterization (electrochemistry, magnetism, electrical transport at the micro- and nanoscales and diverse scanning probe techniques, such as AFM and STM). His research interests also include the physics of strongly correlated systems with novel and exotic functionalities, such as superconductivity and metal-insulator transitions, and physical phenomena arising at surfaces and interfaces of the materials.
